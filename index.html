Wednesday, April 7

Today, I watched as SpaceX launched another 60 of its Starlink satellites into orbit. They did so using their Falcon 9 rocket, which carried the satellites into orbit and then landed back on Earth. I still find it incredible that SpaceX has been able to engineer a rocket that can launch into orbit and consistently stick the landing back on Earth to be reused for future missions! Watching the launch reminded me of the Crew Dragon Demo-2 mission that took place last year. It was one of the most awe-inspiring moments for me in recent memory since it was the first time in almost a decade that Americans were launched into space from American soil in almost a decade, and I saw an interesting animation of the mission which you can check out below.

And if you want to know more about that historic mission, I found an episode of a cool NASA podcast that provided even more background information about it. You can listen to it below!
